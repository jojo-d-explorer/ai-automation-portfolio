## 7. Strategic Insights

### Should you prioritize multi-job companies?

**Yes, selectively.** The reasoning:

**The case FOR prioritizing multi-job companies:**
- Multiple openings = multiple entry points into the same org
- Active hiring signals budget, growth, and urgency to fill
- You can reference one role while applying to another ("I noticed you're also hiring for X, which aligns with my Y experience")
- Rejection from one role doesn't close the door — internal referrals between hiring managers happen

**The case for CAUTION:**
- 2 postings at a 111-company database is barely above noise level — it could be coincidental timing
- Jobgether (the only 3+ company) appears to be a **job aggregator platform**, not a direct employer — its 3 listings may represent 3 different client companies
- High volume ≠ high fit. A company posting 5 roles with avg score 70 is lower priority than a single-posting company with score 95

### Priority Action List

1. **Sardine** — Apply to both partnerships roles. Score 88, remote, fintech sector. Strongest signal in the database.
2. **Bjak** — Score both CoS roles immediately. If they score 85+, this becomes a top-3 target.
3. **Jobgether** — Investigate whether this is a direct employer or aggregator. If aggregator, identify the actual hiring companies behind the 3 listings.
4. **CI&T** — Score the Partner Development Manager role. May be relevant.
5. **Binance, Aircall, Qualysoft** — Lower priority for different reasons (geographic, seniority, or technical mismatch).

### The Bigger Picture

With only 7 of 111 companies appearing twice and 0 appearing 3+ times (excluding the possible aggregator), **company repetition is not yet a strong signal in this dataset.** The database is only 12 days old across 3 search batches. As more weeks of data accumulate, genuine repeat-posters and persistent openings will emerge — that's when Times_Seen and company frequency become truly actionable.

**For now, individual job scores remain the strongest prioritization signal**, with company frequency as a useful secondary tiebreaker.
